About Me

I am a medical doctor and researcher with a passion for advancing knowledge in clinical epidemiology, cancer research, and surgical sciences. With years of diverse experience, my journey has been defined by academic distinction, innovative research, and impactful contributions to improve public health and patient outcomes.

Education

I hold a Doctor of Medicine (MD) degree from Tehran University of Medical Sciences, where I conducted my thesis on the clinical characteristics of benign vocal fold lesions. Complementing my clinical training, I completed a Master of Public Health (MPH) training at Iran University of Medical Sciences, with my thesis focusing on the global assessment of breast cancer epidemiology and quality of care.

Professional Experience

Currently, I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in Cao Lab, at Division of Public Health Sciences, Department of Surgery, Washington University in St. Louis, collaborating on cutting-edge research on cancer epidemiology and surgery. I also contribute to the academic activities as a Teaching Assistant in Intermediate Epidemiology Course as a part of the Master of Population Health Sciences program, guiding students in applying statistical methods to complex survey data and statistical analysis of clinical data.

Previously, I held the Research Data Analyst role in the Leeds Institute of Rheumatic and Musculoskeletal Medicine, University of Leeds, where I specialized in data analysis of clinical cohort studies and biomarkers research, and as a Research Fellow in the Non-Communicable Diseases Research Center, at Tehran University of Medical Sciences, leading numerous projects on clinical research, Global Burden of Disease, and public health.

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of clinical epidemiology and surgery, with a focus on cancer prevention, early detection, and outcomes research. Currently, I am dedicated to studying gastrointestinal cancers, leveraging large datasets and real-world evidence to address emerging questions in cancer epidemiology trends.

Achievements

  • Ranked 1st in the MPH entrance exam, Iran University of Medical Sciences.
  • Awarded the Iranian exceptional scholars researcher program award

Collaborations

  • Member of Team PROSPECT and Future Leaders Working Group in Cancer Grand Challenges
  • Associate Member of the American Association for Cancer Research
  • Senior Collaborator of the Global Burden of Disease Study
  • Academic editor and peer-reviewer for several biomedical journals, including PLOS One and Nature Scientific Reports

Volunteer Activities

In addition to my academic pursuits, I am committed to community health. As a volunteer health educator, I am actively participating in programs to promote cancer preventive health behaviors via increasing cancer awareness and screening.

Beyond Work

When not in the lab or classroom, I enjoy travelling, jogging, swimming, biking, and exploring new cuisines by cooking.
